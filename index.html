About

I am a PhD candidate in Geotechnical Engineering with a focus on integrating Artificial Intelligence and data-driven modeling into soil mechanics. My work explores explainable AI models, hybrid optimization (PSO-MLP), and SHAP-based interpretability for predicting soil compressibility and swelling potential. I aim to bridge the gap between traditional geotechnical analysis and modern computational intelligence.

Research Projects

Soil Compression Index Prediction

Developed gradient boosting models (XGBoost, CatBoost, LightGBM) to predict the compression index with SHAP interpretability.

PSO-MLP Hybrid Modeling

Designed a Particle Swarm Optimization–based Multilayer Perceptron for accurate swelling potential estimation.

Explainable AI for Geotechnics

Implemented SHAP and global sensitivity analysis to explain model predictions in soil parameter estimation.

Data Visualization Tools

Created interactive dashboards and visual analytics using Python, Power BI, and Streamlit for soil datasets.
